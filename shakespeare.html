Characteristics of the Renaissance

Renaissance in French means “rebirth”, therefore the renaissance can looked on as the rebirth of humanism. We also made many new discoveries during the renaissance in music, fine arts, philosophy and many other fields.

Fine arts

Sculptors started using older techniques like contrapposto (an Italian term, which is a way to visualize a human standing with all his weight on one foot). Paintings and sculptures also started to focus more on regular people instead of religious themes.

Literature

During the Renaissance, many people started studying Latin and Greek, so that they could translate old books and turn it into new books. The invention of the book press also allowed them to make more literature. Popular categories of literature were Poetry, Religion and politics. One of the most important authors during the Renaissance was William Shakespeare. The texts he wrote during the Renaissance was not attached to religion. He wrote a lot about drama who were not religious, the main focus was not about god anymore. Many of the texts during the Renaissance were about religion, since the people of Britain just went through a reformation they wrote a lot about religion. More specifically the texts were questioning the orthodoxy in the relationship to the church.

Science

The most important advancement made during the Renaissance were made in Italy by Galileo Galilei. He worked on the telescope allowing us to get a better understanding of the universe.

Religion

Protestantism started during the Renaissance, which has a lot to do with authors studying Latin to be able to translate books such as the bible into English so everyone could read the bible. Not just the priests.

John Milton, born in Bread street London 1608. He was a great poet from England, he was known for his huge ambitions and his goal to become the biggest author in the world. John Donne, born in London 22 January 1572, another great poet/lawyer/ priest from England under the renaissance period. He is mostly known for religious and romantic poems. The Flea is his most known work and it is an erotic story. The poem evokes the aphorism carpe diem, which is Latin for "seize the day". Donne encourages the woman to focus on the present day and time versus saving herself for the afterlife. Donne is able to hint at the erotic without explicitly referring to sex, using images such as the flea that "pampered swells" with the blood of the woman.

Shakespeare’s Romeo and Juliet

Romeo and Juliet is a tragically ending love story about two lovers from different families. Along with his play Hamlet, Romeo and Juliet is still very popular to this today, over four centuries after its release. Shakespeare took inspiration and essentially copied some of the plot from Arthur Brook, a contemporary and an English poet, who in turn rewrote and translated an Italian tale.
