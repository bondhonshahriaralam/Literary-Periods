The culture and social life of Renaissance


Roots

The art and the music was the mainly focused area in the renaissance, but where does everything come from? Let me tell you! The culture of the renaissance was originally from Italy and Greece, or correctly said, it was a left over from those countries, well it was not only Italy and Greece, it was mostly covered by the romantic languages(French ,Spanish ,Italian) for example the word “renaissance” comes from French and it means rebirth. It was the time when cultural dominance of religion was getting weaker. Even the rights of a common person was getting better. It was a birth of humanism, the people of renaissance was starting to go to school etc. (Streams in Literary History). The buildings of the renaissance was an important thing, they built a huge amount of churches and excellent buildings like the temple of san Pietro. The social life of the renaissance was an important thing as well; different people had a different daily life.

Class differences

The rich people was mostly living outside the cities, of course in better residents, their way of socializing was going to theatres and hanging out in the taverns while the middle/lower class was working. Obviously, only the rich ones could afford to go to theatres since the theatres were only available during daytime, which means that the people who went to theatres were not working. It was also expensive to go to theatres and do luxury things. Merchants were second highest paid people in the renaissance time; they were often producing wools and ships. Sometimes the merchants could even be richer than nobbles. That depends on the amount of business they did. Like in every time, peasants/slaves had it worst. They lived in small rooms, while merchants had a big house with 4-5 room. They did not have any job insurance so they could be fired for nothing. As I mentioned before, theatre and roll plays were important in the renaissance time. People went daily to see the theatres. It was a huge entertainment. William Shakespeare was a shining star in the time of renaissance, the puritan merchant Gilles Allan was the owner of the globe theatre where William Shakespeare often performed his sketches. People of London rich/poor were watching his plays day and night. Shakespeare had a different style. In that time, the art section was more for the rich people but Shakespeare wrote his stories in easily understood language so everyone could understand. However, there were some plays meant for the rich ones, those were more difficult to understand. The globe had an architecture identical to the colosseum in Rome. Therefore, we can see the connection between the renaissance countries. The globe was not the only famous theatre in the renaissance time, there was another theatre called “The Swan”, it was located in Southwark, London. The Swan was the most visually impressing theatre at its time. Even in The Swan, you could see some Greco-Roman architecture but not much as in the globe theatre.
