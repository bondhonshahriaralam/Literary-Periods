Societal life

The rift between the classes was large during the Victorian era and moving up a class was very rare. The classes were as follows

  • Upper-class, rich and rarely worked.
  • Middle-class, the men had to work all day but afforded a house and food every day.
  • Working-class, underpaid workers with barely enough to eat. Often forcing their children to work despite being paid even less.

The people lucky enough to be born into the upper class lived a life of pleasure and relaxation. They rarely needed to work and had servants to do everything for them. The upper class was a small minority.

Those of the middle-class had a relatively easy life. The men worked all day to support the family while the women took care of the house and the children studied. They had some extra money so they could afford minor luxuries.

Working-class citizens were underpaid workers that often hardly afforded food and housing. The work they performed was often dangerous and difficult. Despite their hard work, they were paid so little that they often had to force their children to work.
