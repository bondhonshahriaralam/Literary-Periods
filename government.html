Intro

Under the renaissance monarchies and politics experienced a rebirth. People were tired of strong ruling monarchies, and the church having almost full control over the people. Because of this a lot of the more noteworthy monarchs under this time were people who often did revolt the system, to rebirth the system that were present under the middle ages. One of the more noteworthy people in this category was Henrik VII. Henrik VIII is known for two things one: having many wife’s and two: Breaking away England from the Catholic church. He did after being tired of the Churchs power over society and the throne; he wanted more power to himself and by breaking away and forming The Church of England. This was a common tread between rulers under the renaissance as a revolution of thought were sweeping through countries having a big effect for politics under that time, politics in general became more secular, more liberal.

Political Philosophy

Thomas Hobbes was an English philosopher that is seen as one of the fathers of political philosophy. In typical renaissance style, he wrote a lot about the right of the individual; the natural equality of all men. In his most famous, book Leviathan. He discusses everything from what humans want, how a society should be run and the problems facing society. He was of course inspired by a lot of the French political philosophers at the time.

Elizabeth I

Elizabeth I was the daughter of Henry VIII and took power of the throne in 1558, she reestablished the protestant faith after Mary 1 had changed it back to Catholicism and it being highly unpopular. She never got married and focused a lot on making England strong, protecting its people and making the middle class strong. She didn’t try to conquer other countries and avoided war. She was highly popular and some would even call the best monarch to ever hold the throne. She was also a huge fan of Shakespeare’s plays and enjoyed them often.
